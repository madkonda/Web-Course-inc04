Chapter one

Love's Beginning

On an ordinary February evening, the city streets were washed in soft ‚ô• neon pinks and purples. Two strangers walked the same path every day, eyes on their phones, hearts a little too busy to notice each other.

Tonight, Cupid decided the routine had gone on long enough. He drew a glowing arc through the air, loosing an arrow that traced a perfect curve between their worlds. A single snowflake-heart drifted down, landing on both sleeves at the exact same moment.

Their eyes met. The noise of the street softened. In a heartbeat, the city felt less like a crowd and more like a stage built for just two people.

Sometimes love doesn't arrive with fireworks—just a shared smile under a soft pink sky.

Chapter two

Cupid's Garden

Days later, they met again in a hidden rooftop garden where vines curled around railings like handwritten poetry. Lanterns swayed in the evening breeze, each one carrying a tiny, flickering heart.

Cupid watched from the branches above, gently nudging petals into the air. With every laugh they shared, roses along the path opened a little wider, as if the garden itself were listening.

They traded stories, then secrets, then the quiet kind of silence that only feels possible when someone finally feels like home.

Love grows best where we feel safe enough to bloom.

  • Lanterns or fairy lights with a soft glow

  • Cupid perched on a branch, wings gently fluttering

Chapter three

Eternal Romance

Seasons turned. The city traded snow for blossoms, blossoms for warm summer rain. But the rooftop garden stayed the same: a small, constant chapter above an ever-changing story.

Under a sky scattered with stars, they returned to the place where their paths first crossed on purpose. Cupid didn't need arrows anymore—every glance, every shared memory, every quiet promise was proof that his work was done.

As they traced their names in a heart on the fogged glass, the city lights below shimmered like they were clapping for a happy ending.

Eternal love isn't about never changing—it's about choosing each other again, in every new chapter.
